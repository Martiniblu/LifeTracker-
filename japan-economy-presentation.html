01 · The Hook
Japan just ended 30 years of monetary history — in 12 months.

From negative interest rates to the highest policy rate since 1995. This isn't a tweak — it's a structural reset.

Takeaway — Japan's economic playbook has been rewritten. Your strategy should be too.
01 / 07
02 · The Problem
"Free money" for a decade created dangerous dependency.
  • Negative interest rates propped up "zombie" firms while crushing bank margins for 7+ years.
  • Nominal wages stagnated for three decades even as asset prices soared under Abenomics.
  • Real GDP grew just 0.1% in 2024 — near-zero growth, not the recovery the policy promised.
Takeaway — Ultra-loose policy solved deflation but created structural fragility across every sector.
02 / 07
03 · The Insight
The rate hike isn't a threat — it's Japan's biggest opportunity in a generation.
  • Wage growth hit 5.46% in 2026 — levels unseen since 1991 — igniting real consumer spending power.
  • Record ¥29.3 trillion current account surplus and US$31 billion in greenfield FDI hit in 2024 alone.
  • Normalisation is forcing capital toward productive sectors: semiconductors, AI infrastructure, healthcare.
Takeaway — Tightening is flushing out dead capital and redirecting it toward Japan's next-growth sectors.
03 / 07
04 · The Solution
Four transmission channels are reshaping where capital wins.
Interest-Rate Channel
Rising borrowing costs kill zombie firms; regional banks finally earn margins again.
Wage–Price Channel
Shunto wage anchor: 5.1% → 5.42% → 5.46% over three consecutive years.
Corporate-Governance Channel
TSE pressure on below-book-value firms: Nikkei surpassed its 1989 bubble peak.
FDI Channel
Japan repositioned as supply-chain resilience hub: TSMC Kumamoto leads the wave.
Takeaway — Winners will be firms aligned with rising wages, domestic demand, and hard-asset infrastructure.

06 · The Risk
One threat could derail normalisation: fiscal policy.
  • Public debt sits at ~230% of GDP — every 25bp rate hike amplifies debt-servicing costs measurably.
  • FY2026 budget hit ¥122.3T with a ¥21.3T stimulus package, pushing 10-year JGB yields above 2%.
  • CPI already slipped to 1.5% in May 2026 — below the BoJ's own 2% target — yet hikes continued.
Takeaway — Monitor fiscal trajectory: monetary success can be undone by an expansionary government.

Reposition your Japan strategy for normalisation — now.
Three actions before year-end
  • 1 Audit your capital structure — leverage that was free in 2023 carries real cost at 1%+ rates.
  • 2 Shift sector exposure toward domestic demand: premium goods, healthcare, fintech, automation.

Takeaway — The window for repositioning is open now. The firms that move first will define Japan 3.0.
